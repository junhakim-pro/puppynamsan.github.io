Getting Started: From Airport to City Center

Your journey begins at Incheon International Airport (ICN). You have two great train options to get into Seoul:

  • AREX Express Train: The fastest way. It runs non-stop from Incheon to Seoul Station in about 45 minutes. Perfect if you're staying near the city center.
  • AREX All-Stop Train: A bit slower (about 60 minutes) as it makes several stops, but it connects directly to other subway lines, which might be more convenient depending on your destination.

Must-Do: Get a T-money Card

Before you even get on the train, buy a T-money card. This is your rechargeable transportation card. You can buy and top it up at any convenience store (like 7-Eleven, GS25) or at the ticket vending machines inside every subway station. You simply tap the card on the reader when you enter and exit the gates. It's cheaper than single-use tickets and works on buses too!

Riding the Subway: The Basics

Navigating the stations is surprisingly easy. Everything is color-coded and signed in English.

  • Follow the Colors and Numbers: Each line has a unique color and number (e.g., Line 2 is the green circle line). Signs on the walls and floors will guide you to your platform.
  • Check Your Direction: Before going down to the platform, check the signs that list the major stations in each direction. Your map app will tell you the name of the final station in your direction of travel (e.g., "Towards Gangnam").
  • Transfers are Easy: To transfer, just follow the "Transfer to Line X" signs. The apps will even tell you how long the walk will be. Tap out and tap back in only when the signs explicitly tell you to.

5

Pro Tips for a Smooth Ride

  • Use the free Wi-Fi: Most subway cars have free Wi-Fi available.
  • Mind the designated seating: There are seats reserved for the elderly, pregnant women, and disabled passengers at the ends of each car. Avoid using them unless you need to.
  • Stand clear of the doors: People will rush in and out, so it's best to stand to the side while waiting.
  • Stay quiet: It's considered polite to keep conversations low and avoid talking on the phone while on the train.
  • Pay attention to the last train: Service usually ends around midnight. Your app will tell you the last train time for your route.
